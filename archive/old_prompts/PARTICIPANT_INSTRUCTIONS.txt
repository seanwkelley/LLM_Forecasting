FORECASTING STUDY — PARTICIPANT INSTRUCTIONS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

STUDY OVERVIEW

You will analyze a series of intelligence briefings about an ongoing geopolitical
crisis. Your task is to forecast whether the smaller power's government will
collapse or be removed from power.

You will work with an AI assistant to analyze the information and make forecasts.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
INFORMATION AVAILABLE
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

You will receive information that would be publicly available to news agencies,
think tanks, and professional forecasters:

  [OK] Observable military movements and deployments
  [OK] Public diplomatic statements and negotiations
  [OK] Economic sanctions and trade actions
  [OK] Verified external events (e.g., protests, leadership changes)
  [OK] Public indicators (territory, crisis level, international support)

You will NOT have access to:

  [FAIL] Internal government deliberations
  [FAIL] Private diplomatic communications
  [FAIL] Classified intelligence
  [FAIL] Strategic planning discussions

This constraint mirrors real-world forecasting conditions.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
HOW TO MAKE FORECASTS
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

For each period, you will:

1. READ the intelligence briefing carefully

2. DISCUSS with your AI assistant:
   • What are the key indicators?
   • How have conditions changed since last period?
   • What factors increase/decrease collapse probability?
   • What are relevant historical precedents?

3. PROVIDE your forecast:
   • Probability (0-100%) of government collapse
   • Confidence level (Low/Medium/High)
   • Brief rationale (2-3 sentences)

TIPS FOR GOOD FORECASTING
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

• Consider base rates: Historically, how often do governments collapse in
  similar conflicts?

• Weight multiple factors: Military, economic, diplomatic, and domestic
  stability indicators

• Track trends: Is the situation improving or deteriorating?

• Be calibrated: If you say 70%, you should be correct about 70% of the time

• Update beliefs: Adjust your forecast as new information arrives

• Use your AI assistant: They can help analyze patterns, retrieve relevant
  context, and check your reasoning
