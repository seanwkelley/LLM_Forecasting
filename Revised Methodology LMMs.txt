Revised Methodology: Structural Grounding in LLMs1. Primary Statistical Framework (The Paper)To provide a high-powered, frequentist proof of structural grounding, we move from per-question correlations to a Linear Mixed-Effects (LME) regression. This approach utilizes the full variance of the continuous topological data while controlling for model-specific "temperament" and question-level volatility.Model A: Node Importance (Variable Hierarchy)We model the absolute probability shift ($|\Delta|$) as a function of the node's Betweenness Centrality, $z$-scored within each question to normalize for graph size (4–8 nodes).$$|\Delta|_{mqi} = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot \text{Importance}_{mqi}^{(z)} + \gamma_m \cdot \text{Model}_m + ( \text{Importance}_{mqi}^{(z)} | \text{Question}_q ) + \varepsilon_{mqi}$$$\beta_1$ (Fixed Effect): Represents the "Global Grounding" coefficient—the average shift increase per unit of centrality.$\gamma_m$ (Model Fixed Effect): Captures model-specific baselines (e.g., GPT-4o being more "jumpy" vs. Gemini being more "stubborn").Random Slope $(| \text{Question}_q)$: Accounts for domain-specific variance (e.g., Finance graphs vs. Geopolitics).Model B: Edge Importance (Topological Flow)We model edge sensitivity separately to isolate the model's understanding of causal "wiring" from its understanding of variable "importance."$$|\Delta| \sim \text{PathRelevance}^{(z)} + \text{Model}_{FE} + (\text{PathRelevance}^{(z)} | \text{Question})$$2. Validation: The Scrambled Edge "Placebo"To prove the effect is mechanistically tied to the DAG topology, we run a separate LME on the Scrambled Edge condition.Hypothesis: The grounding coefficient ($\beta_1$) should significantly degrade or trend toward zero when the edges no longer support the node's theoretical centrality.Advantage: This replaces the side-by-side $\tau$ comparison with a formal comparison of regression slopes.3. Interactive Diagnostic (The Causal Forecast Lab)For the user-facing tool, we prioritize interpretability over statistical abstraction. We utilize the Structural Sensitivity Ratio (SSR), restricted to Nodes Only.The Metric: Node-Only SSR$$SSR = \frac{\text{Mean } |\Delta| \text{ of High-Importance Nodes}}{\text{Mean } |\Delta| \text{ of Low-Importance Nodes}}$$Interpretation: An $SSR > 1.0$ indicates the model is "Grounded" (prioritizing bottlenecks).UI Integration: Displayed as a "Structural Integrity Grade," providing users with an immediate ratio of how much more the LLM weights core causes versus peripheral details in their specific generated graph.4. Visual StrategyThe final results section will utilize a three-panel figure to replace redundant categorical checks:Panel A (The Taxonomy): A hierarchical bar chart of mean shifts across all intervention types (Strengthen, Negate, Spurious, Control).Panel B (The Mechanism): An LME Predicted Effects plot showing the Grounding Slopes for each model (X: Importance, Y: Predicted $|\Delta|$).Panel C (The Topology): A scatter plot showing the correlation between Path Relevance and shift magnitude for edge probes.
