# Faction Objectives - Pre-Invasion Scenario

**Scenario Context:** Novaris has massed 180,000 troops on the border. Invasion is possible but NOT inevitable. All factions face critical choices before shots are fired.

---

## 🔴 Novaris (Major Power - Aggressor)

**Strategic Position:**
- Military mobilization 40% complete and ongoing (decision point approaching)
- Economic vulnerabilities: Energy export dependent (30% revenue via Tethys transit routes)
- Cyber infrastructure critical but exposed to attack
- Internal opposition: 15% ethnic Tethyan population + democratic activist movements
- Multiple strategic options available across all domains

**Objectives:**
1. **Primary:** Prevent Tethys from joining Meridian Alliance (Western integration)
2. **Secondary:** "Reunify" historic territory, protect ethnic Novarans in Tethys
3. **Tertiary:** Demonstrate strength, resist Western "encirclement"

**Can achieve objectives through:**
- **Military:** Continue buildup, limited strikes, border operations, or full invasion
- **Economic:** Energy cutoffs, sanctions, trade restrictions, currency warfare
- **Cyber:** Attack Tethys infrastructure, steal intelligence, disrupt mobilization
- **Covert:** Regime change operations, support Tethys opposition, sabotage, assassinations
- **Diplomatic:** Negotiate settlement, threaten consequences, split Western coalition

**Internal Debate:**
- **Hawks argue:** Delay only strengthens Tethys - strike now before mobilization complete
- **Doves warn:** Invasion risks quagmire, international isolation, and Tethys can impose costs
- **Question:** Can objectives be achieved without unacceptable losses?

**Vulnerabilities Tethys could exploit:**
- Energy transit routes (30% of export revenue flows through Tethys pipelines)
- Internal opposition (ethnic Tethyans + democrats could be supported)
- Cyber infrastructure (financial systems, power grid exposed)
- Economic dependencies (sanctions-susceptible, currency vulnerable)
- Mobilization centers (exposed to precision strikes)

**Key Dilemma:** Tethys's Western orientation is unacceptable, but Tethys is NOT helpless - escalation carries real risks.

---

## 🔵 Tethys (Small Power - BUT NOT HELPLESS)

**Strategic Position:**
- **Military:** Smaller but capable - precision strike capacity, strong cyber units, can impose costs
- **Economic:** Controls critical energy transit routes (30% of Novaris export revenue)
- **Intelligence:** Ethnic Tethyan networks inside Novaris (15% of Novaris population), strong counterintelligence
- **Asymmetric advantages:** Can make invasion extremely costly, insurgency preparation ready, international sympathy
- **Multiple strategic options** - NOT limited to defense and diplomacy

**Objectives:**
1. **Primary:** Prevent invasion OR make it too costly to sustain
2. **Secondary:** Secure Meridian Alliance membership (Western protection guarantee)
3. **Tertiary:** Maintain sovereignty and territorial integrity

**Can achieve objectives through:**
- **Deterrence:** Threaten costly strikes on Novaris infrastructure, transit shutdowns, cyber attacks
- **Preemption:** Strike mobilization centers first, disrupt invasion preparations, eliminate key targets
- **Economic warfare:** Shut down energy transit routes, asset seizures, trade embargoes, currency attacks
- **Covert operations:** Support Novaris opposition movements, sabotage inside Novaris, assassinate leaders
- **Cyber warfare:** Attack Novaris power grid, financial systems, government networks, military communications
- **Diplomacy:** Rally Western allies, negotiate settlement, secure protection guarantees, build coalitions
- **Insurgency preparation:** Make occupation cost-prohibitive through prepared resistance networks

**Internal Debate:**
- **Hawks argue:** Strike first to disrupt mobilization - waiting guarantees invasion. Show Novaris the cost.
- **Doves warn:** Escalation plays into Novaris hands - use time to rally allies and negotiate
- **Question:** Can we deter through credible threat of costly resistance, or must we strike preemptively?

**Offensive Capabilities:**
- **Cyber units:** Can attack Novaris power grid, financial sector, government systems
- **Precision strikes:** Can hit Novaris border mobilization centers, supply depots, command posts
- **Economic leverage:** Control of transit pipelines (can cut 30% of Novaris revenue)
- **Intelligence networks:** Ethnic Tethyans in Novaris enable sabotage, intelligence gathering, opposition support
- **Covert operations:** Can assassinate Novaris leaders, sabotage infrastructure, spread disinformation
- **Asymmetric warfare:** Prepared insurgency networks make occupation extremely costly

**Key Dilemma:** Deterrence requires credible threat of costs - but demonstrating capability risks provoking the invasion we're trying to prevent.

---

## 🟢 Meridian (Allied to Defender)

**Strategic Position:**
- Tethys seeking alliance membership (trigger for crisis)
- Must balance deterrence with escalation risk
- Alliance credibility at stake

**Objectives:**
1. **Primary:** Deter Novaris invasion without triggering war
2. **Secondary:** Support Tethys membership bid (expand alliance)
3. **Tertiary:** Maintain alliance credibility without catastrophic escalation

**Internal Debate:**
- **Hawks argue:** Clear defense commitment will deter - ambiguity invites testing
- **Doves warn:** Explicit guarantees risk being dragged into war
- **Question:** What level of support deters without provoking?

**Constraints:**
- Domestic opposition to foreign wars
- Economic interdependence with Novaris (energy, trade)
- Other global commitments

**Key Dilemma:** Strategic ambiguity vs explicit commitment - which prevents war?

---

## 🟠 Valkoria (Allied to Aggressor)

**Strategic Position:**
- Strategic partner with Novaris (Treaty of Eternal Friendship)
- Wants Novaris to succeed without prolonged war
- Isolated from West due to sanctions

**Objectives:**
1. **Primary:** Support Novaris goals without direct military involvement
2. **Secondary:** Break Western sanctions regime / prevent "color revolution" contagion
3. **Tertiary:** Preserve authoritarian governance model

**Internal Debate:**
- **Hawks argue:** Show strength - Tethyan capitulation validates our model
- **Moderates counsel:** Use threat but seek negotiated outcome
- **Question:** How to help Novaris achieve objectives without costly war?

**Constraints:**
- Economic dependence on Novaris
- Already under Western sanctions (limited to lose)
- Domestic vulnerabilities (demographic decline, brain drain)

**Key Dilemma:** Novaris has legitimate grievances, but prolonged conflict costly for both allies.

---

## 🟡 Aurelia (Neutral Mediator)

**Strategic Position:**
- Internally divided (energy dependent on Novaris, values aligned with West)
- Scrambling to prevent war
- Can offer mediation as "honest broker"

**Objectives:**
1. **Primary:** Prevent war (catastrophic for all, including Aurelia)
2. **Secondary:** Find diplomatic solution before window closes
3. **Tertiary:** Propose security frameworks acceptable to both sides

**Internal Debate:**
- **Idealists:** Must stand with democracy / rule of law
- **Pragmatists:** Energy dependence constrains options
- **Question:** Can Aurelia broker a deal that gives both sides a way out?

**Constraints:**
- Energy dependence on Novaris (40% of natural gas)
- Internal political divisions
- Limited leverage over either party

**Key Dilemma:** Managing internal divisions while keeping diplomatic channels open.

---

## 🔵 International Organization

**Strategic Position:**
- Neutral facilitation role
- Critical window to prevent conflict
- Limited enforcement power

**Objectives:**
1. **Primary:** Prevent outbreak of war (prevention easier than resolution)
2. **Secondary:** Provide neutral platform for dialogue
3. **Tertiary:** Propose confidence-building measures / security guarantees

**Internal Debate:**
- **Pragmatists:** Both sides have legitimate concerns - find middle ground
- **Principalists:** Cannot reward threats of force with concessions
- **Question:** What framework addresses core concerns without legitimizing coercion?

**Constraints:**
- No enforcement mechanism
- Dependent on major powers for funding/legitimacy
- Must maintain impartiality

**Key Dilemma:** War is not inevitable - there is still time, but once fighting starts, mediation becomes much harder.

## 🎯 Conflict Dynamics

**Aligned Interests:**
- Novaris ↔ Valkoria: Prevent Western expansion, support each other
- Tethys ↔ Meridian: Deter aggression, defend sovereignty
- Aurelia ↔ Int'l Org: Find diplomatic solution, prevent war

**Competing Interests:**
- Novaris vs Tethys: Zero-sum on sovereignty/alliance membership
- Meridian vs Valkoria: Proxy confrontation through their allies
- Aurelia: Torn between values (support Tethys) and interests (energy from Novaris)

**Wild Cards:**
- **Deterrence paradox:** Strong Meridian commitment might prevent OR provoke war
- **Concession dilemma:** Tethyan concessions might satisfy OR embolden Novaris
- **Time pressure:** Every day of tension increases risk of miscalculation/accident
- **Internal divisions:** Hawks vs doves within EACH faction
