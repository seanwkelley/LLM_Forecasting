# Faction Objectives Review - Major and Small Powers

**Date:** February 1, 2026
**Scenario:** Pre-Invasion Crisis (v3.8.1)

---

## Overview

This document reviews the strategic objectives and goals of the two main powers in the simulation: **Novaris (major_power)** and **Tethys (small_power)**. These objectives drive agent decision-making and shape the conflict dynamics.

---

## NOVARIS (major_power) - The Aggressor

### Strategic Position
- **Military Strength:** Superior (major power)
- **Leadership:** Minister Dmitri Volkov (Realist, 68% hawk)
- **Current Status:** Poised to act but hasn't invaded yet

### Core Objectives (Pre-Invasion Scenario)

**Primary Goal:** Prevent Tethys from joining Meridian Alliance

**Strategic Options Available:**
1. **Invasion** - Full military operation to occupy/control Tethys
2. **Continued Pressure** - Coercion without invasion (preferred if it works)
3. **Negotiation** - Diplomatic settlement preserving core interests

**Key Decision Criteria:**
- Can objectives be achieved at acceptable cost?
- Will delay strengthen or weaken Tethys?
- Can sanctions/international pressure be withstood?

### How Novaris Frames the Conflict

**"Defensive Necessity, Not Aggression"**

1. **Historical Legitimacy**
   - Tethys was part of Novaris Empire for 208 years (1742-1950)
   - "Independence" was an accident during Novaris's weakness
   - Reunification is "restoration of historical justice"

2. **Existential Security Threat**
   - Meridian Alliance expansion toward borders
   - Tethys joining West = encirclement
   - Must be stopped before it's too late

3. **Protection of Ethnic Novarans**
   - 28% of Tethys population is ethnic Novaran
   - Claims of persecution (rejected by international observers)
   - "Duty" to protect co-ethnics

4. **Anti-Western Narrative**
   - Western criticism is hypocritical
   - Rules-based order serves Western interests
   - Standing up for sovereignty against hegemony

**Domestic Framing:** "Liberation" and "Historical Restoration" - most Novaris citizens genuinely believe this

### Acceptable Outcomes (Ranked)

**Best Case:**
- Tethys abandons Western integration
- Neutral or Novaris-aligned buffer state
- Achieved through pressure/negotiation (low cost)

**Acceptable:**
- Limited military operation secures objectives
- Quick victory, manageable costs
- International pressure withstandable

**Tolerable:**
- Prolonged conflict but eventual success
- Higher costs but strategic goals met

**Unacceptable:**
- Tethys joins Meridian Alliance
- Novaris forced to accept Western-aligned Tethys
- Quagmire that destabilizes Novaris leadership

### Internal Debate

**Hawks (General Krasnov, 92% hawk):**
- Delay only strengthens Tethys
- Strike now while advantage exists
- Diplomacy without force is meaningless

**Doves (Dr. Petrova, 25% hawk):**
- Economic costs are severe
- Sanctions biting harder than admitted
- Prolonged conflict risks economic collapse

**Pragmatists (Volkov, 68% hawk):**
- Calculate cost-benefit
- Use force if necessary
- But prefer achieving goals at lower cost

### What Success Looks Like

**Strategic Victory:**
1. Tethys does NOT join Meridian Alliance
2. Novaris security buffer preserved
3. Regional hegemony restored
4. Acceptable costs (economic, military, political)

**Political Victory (Volkov's personal stakes):**
- Quick, decisive outcome cements his position
- Prolonged quagmire could end his career
- Must balance military hawks and economic doves

## TETHYS (small_power) - The Defender

### Strategic Position
- **Military Strength:** Inferior (small power, 0.60 vs Novaris 1.00)
- **Leadership:** President Elena Marchetti (Liberal Institutionalist, 62% hawk)
- **Current Status:** Facing invasion threat, not yet invaded

### Core Objectives (Pre-Invasion Scenario)

**Primary Goal:** Prevent invasion while preserving sovereignty

**Strategic Dilemma:**
- How much should be conceded to avoid war?
- How much is too much (crossing into surrender)?
- When does compromise become submission?

**Key Questions:**
- Can diplomacy prevent invasion?
- Will international support be enough?
- Is resistance futile or necessary?

### How Tethys Frames the Conflict

**"Unprovoked Aggression Against Sovereign Democracy"**

1. **Independence is Legitimate**
   - Earned through 1950 struggle
   - 76 years of independence
   - Right to choose own alliances and future

2. **International Law is Clear**
   - Territorial integrity must be respected
   - Sovereignty is inviolable
   - Aggression violates rules-based order

3. **Novaris Claims are Pretexts**
   - "Protection" narrative is imperialism
   - Ethnic Novarans not persecuted
   - Historical claims don't justify conquest

4. **Existential Stakes**
   - Surrender = end of Tethys as independent nation
   - Not just territory - national existence
   - Fight is for survival itself

**Public Support:** Most Tethyans support continued resistance, viewing alternative as national extinction

### Acceptable Outcomes (Ranked)

**Best Case:**
- Invasion deterred through diplomacy
- Sovereignty preserved
- Western integration proceeds
- Security guarantees obtained

**Acceptable:**
- Some concessions made
- But independence preserved
- Core sovereignty intact
- Breathing room gained

**Tolerable:**
- War occurs but Tethys survives
- International support materializes
- Resistance sustainable
- eventual negotiated settlement

**Unacceptable:**
- Surrender of sovereignty
- Puppet government
- Novaris occupation
- National extinction

### Internal Debate

**Hawks (General Bondar, 88% hawk):**
- Must hit Novaris hard early
- Only strength deters aggression
- Concessions show weakness

**Doves (Minister Kovalenko, 32% hawk):**
- War means catastrophic losses
- Some compromise necessary
- Diplomacy still possible

**Liberal Institutionalists (Marchetti, 62% hawk):**
- International law matters
- Western support is coming
- Stand firm on principles but prepare for reality

### What Success Looks Like

**Strategic Victory:**
1. Invasion prevented or defeated
2. Sovereignty preserved
3. Western integration secured
4. International support sustained

**Survival:**
1. Independent Tethys still exists
2. Government still functions
3. Territory mostly intact
4. Path to recovery exists

**Political Victory (Marchetti's stakes):**
- Defiance vindicates her platform
- International symbol of resistance
- But learning wartime leadership on the job
- Sometimes underestimates gap between legal rights and military realities

## Comparing the Two Powers

### Fundamental Conflict

| Aspect | Novaris (major_power) | Tethys (small_power) |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Goal** | Control/neutralize Tethys | Preserve independence |
| **Frame** | "Historical restoration" | "Defending sovereignty" |
| **Stakes** | Regional hegemony | National existence |
| **Constraint** | Economic costs, international pressure | Military inferiority |
| **Strategy** | Pressure → invasion if needed | Deter → resist if necessary |
| **Support** | Valkoria backing | Meridian support (but limits) |
| **Legitimacy** | Historical claims | International law |
| **Risk** | Quagmire, isolation | Defeat, occupation |

### Key Asymmetries

**Military:**
- Novaris: Superior force (1.00 vs 0.60)
- Tethys: Defensive advantage, international weapons

**Economic:**
- Novaris: Larger economy, but sanctions hurt
- Tethys: Smaller but Western-backed

**Political:**
- Novaris: Regime stability, but economic pressure
- Tethys: Democratic legitimacy, international sympathy

**Time Pressure:**
- Novaris: Hawks say "act now before window closes"
- Tethys: "Each day without invasion is victory"

### Zero-Sum Elements

These objectives are **fundamentally incompatible:**

1. **Alliance Membership**
   - Novaris: Tethys CANNOT join Meridian Alliance
   - Tethys: Sovereign right to choose alliances

2. **Sovereignty**
   - Novaris: Tethys as part of "sphere of influence"
   - Tethys: Full independence and self-determination

3. **Historical Narrative**
   - Novaris: "Restoration of historical unity"
   - Tethys: "76 years of earned independence"

**No middle ground exists on core issues** - any settlement requires one side to accept partial defeat.

## Decision-Making Implications

### For Novaris (major_power)

**Multi-Action System Enables:**
- **Military pressure** (troop movements, show of force)
- **Covert operations** (sabotage, proxy support)
- **Economic leverage** (embargoes, trade restrictions)
- **Diplomatic tracks** (negotiations, backchannel talks)

**Strategic Coherence:**
Minister Volkov (realist) tries to:
- Maximize pressure on Tethys
- Minimize costs to Novaris
- Preserve strategic flexibility
- Balance hawks and doves internally

**Action Patterns:**
- Approve offensive military actions (deterrence)
- Approve covert operations (plausible deniability)
- Counter excessive provocation (avoid overheating)
- Upgrade diplomatic visibility (signal credibility)

### For Tethys (small_power)

**Multi-Action System Enables:**
- **Defensive military** (buildup, fortification, deterrence)
- **Intelligence gathering** (reconnaissance, not offense)
- **Diplomatic engagement** (talks, international support)
- **Economic pressure** (embargoes on aggressor)

**Strategic Coherence:**
President Marchetti (liberal institutionalist) tries to:
- Deter invasion through visible defense
- Maintain moral high ground (norm compliance)
- Rally international support
- Avoid provocations that justify invasion

**Action Patterns:**
- Approve defensive military actions
- VETO norm-violating operations (sabotage, cyber attacks, false flags)
- Counter kinetic strikes to non-lethal alternatives
- Approve lawful economic pressure

## Recommendations

### Current Objectives Are Well-Balanced

The simulation correctly models:
1. **Asymmetric conflict** - different resources, different stakes
2. **Incompatible goals** - genuine strategic dilemma
3. **Internal tensions** - hawks vs doves within each faction
4. **Worldview-driven decisions** - leaders act according to beliefs

### Potential Enhancements (Optional)

**1. More Explicit Victory Conditions**

Add to prompts:
```
YOUR FACTION'S VICTORY CONDITIONS:
- [Specific measurable outcomes]
- [Red lines that cannot be crossed]
- [Acceptable compromises]
```

**2. Evolving Objectives**

As crisis progresses:
- Novaris: Initial goals → adjusted for costs
- Tethys: Prevent invasion → survive invasion → negotiate settlement

**3. Personal vs Factional Objectives**

Already implemented but could be emphasized:
- Volkov: Faction goals + personal career survival
- Marchetti: Faction goals + principled leadership
